About

Lifelong Hobby...

The coding bug bit me early, when in fourth grade I built an informational website on the Revolutionary War with two classmates. The graphics were in Paint and the CSS was nonexistent, but the experience was enough to get me hooked. Throughout middle school, I developed gymnast fan sites and maintained a personal blog using HTML, CSS, and PHP. Although coding websites remained a hobby, it never developed into more as I pursued other academic interests, studying economics and international relations in college/grad school.

...Turned True Career Aspiration

But time and again I felt myself coming back to programming, jumping at the opportunity to overhaul the website of the nonprofit organization I was working for, taking on small freelance projects, and diving into various Codecademy lessons. In May 2014, I took the plunge, enrolling in an Accelerated Programmers Training program at the local community college. The certificate program provided an excellent foundation in OOP in Python/Java and relational databases, which I supplemented throughout the year with outside learning resources from local Meetups to organized hackathons.

While I continue to learn and improve every day, currently working through the MEAN stack curriculum at FreeCodeCamp.com, I am excited -- and most importantly ready -- to take this full-time.
